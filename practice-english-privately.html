Why this feels different

Most speaking anxiety isn't about English. It's about being watched.

It's hard to take a risk with your words when someone's reaction is also on the line. Take the audience away, and the language gets a lot easier to practice.

No real person to disappoint

You can repeat the same sentence five times, mispronounce a word badly, or completely freeze mid-thought — and nothing about it follows you into your next real conversation.

No performance, just practice

There's no class watching, no colleague judging, no awkward pause while someone waits for you to find the word. It's just you and a patient AI partner, on repeat.

Corrections without the cringe

Feedback shows up as a simple, specific note — never a tone of disappointment, never a sigh. The kind of correction you can actually absorb instead of shrink from.

Rehearse before it counts

Run through your job interview, your doctor's appointment, or your phone call five times in private before you ever have to do it once for real.

How a private session goes

Small enough steps that starting doesn't feel like a big deal.

1

Open the app, no setup

No profile to build, no account to create. Pick a topic that feels low-stakes — your day, a hobby, anything.

2

Speak at your own pace

Pause as long as you need. Restart your sentence. The AI waits — it's not going anywhere, and nothing's being timed against you.

3

Get a gentle correction

A short, specific note on what to adjust — phrased plainly, never as a judgment on you.

4

Try again, immediately

Say it again with the fix. That repetition, done privately, is what starts to make the words feel like yours.

Before you start

What people usually ask

Is my voice recording saved anywhere?

No. Your voice is converted to text directly on your device using Android's built-in speech recognition. The raw audio itself is never uploaded to any server.

Does anyone else see my conversations?

No. There's no account system and no server-side database of what you've said. Everything lives in an encrypted local database on your own phone, and stays there until you choose to clear it.

What if I make a lot of mistakes?

That's what the space is for. You'll get specific, plain-spoken corrections with no judgment attached — and nothing about a difficult session is visible to anyone but you.

Do I need to be good at English to start?

No. SpeakMate is built for exactly the opposite — for the moment before you feel ready, which is usually the moment that matters most.

You don't have to feel ready. You just have to start.
